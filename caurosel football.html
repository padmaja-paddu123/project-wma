RULES OF Foot ball

RULES

Gameplay Basics The ball must be moved using any body part except hands and arms, except for the goalkeeper within their designated area ¹ ². Players can use their feet, chest, or head to control and move the ball.

Scoring A goal is scored when the ball completely crosses the goal line between the goalposts and under the crossbar ¹. The team with the most goals at the end of the 90 minutes wins.

1.Common Rules Some key rules to keep in mind: - Offside: A player is offside if they're closer to the opponent's goal line than both the ball and the second-to-last opponent when the ball is played forward ¹ ². - Fouls: Physical contact or unsportsmanlike behavior can result in a free kick or penalty kick for the opposing team ¹ ². - Free Kicks: Awarded after a foul, allowing the opposing team to kick the ball from where the foul occurred ¹. - Penalty Kicks: Awarded after a serious foul within the goalkeeper's area, allowing a shot on goal ¹. - Goalkeepers: Can use hands within their designated area, but must follow specific rules ¹ ².
2.Gameplay Stoppage The game can be stopped due to various reasons, including: - Ball out of bounds: When the ball crosses the touchline or goal line ¹. - Fouls: When a player commits a foul, resulting in a free kick or penalty kick ¹ ². - Injuries: When a player is injured, and the referee stops play ¹.
3.Restarts The game can be restarted with: - Kick-offs: Starting or restarting play from the center spot ¹. - Throw-ins: When the ball goes out of bounds over the touchline ¹. - Goal kicks: When the ball goes out of bounds over the goal line and was last touched by an opponent ¹. - Corner kicks: When the ball goes out of bounds over the goal line and was last touched by the defending team ¹.
