RULES OF TICKET TO RIDE





Ticket to Ride is a popular train-themed board game. Here are the rules: Number of Players: 2-5 players. Objective: Complete Destination Tickets (routes) by claiming train routes on the map.

Components: 1. Game board featuring a map of North America. 2. 225 Colored Train Cars (55 each of blue, green, yellow, black, and red). 3. 140 Train Car Cards. 4. 30 Destination Ticket Cards. 5. 5 Scoring Markers. 6. 1 Rulebook. Setup: 1. Place the game board in the middle. 2. Shuffle and deal 5 Train Car Cards to each player. 3. Each player chooses 4 Destination Ticket Cards. 4. Remaining cards are placed within reach.



Gameplay: Rounds: The game consists of multiple rounds. Turns: On each player's turn: 1. Draw Train Car Cards: Draw 2 cards from the deck or 1 face-up card. 2. Claim Route: Use colored Train Car Cards to claim a route on the map. 3. Draw Destination Tickets: Draw 3 new tickets.

Claiming Routes: 1. Choose a route on the map. 2. Play colored Train Car Cards matching the route's color. 3. Place colored Train Cars on the route.



Destination Tickets: 1. Complete routes match Destination Tickets. 2. Keep tickets secret.

Endgame: 1. Last player draws Train Car Cards. 2. Players reveal completed Destination Tickets. 3. Score points.

Scoring: 1. Completed Destination Tickets: Score points listed. 2. Longest Continuous Path: 10 points. 3. Uncompleted Tickets: - points listed. 4. Total points determine the winner.



Rules: 1. Route Limits: No more than 2 players can claim the same route. 2. Train Car Limits: 45 Train Cars per player. 3. Destination Ticket Limits: 5 tickets per player.



Common Terms: 1. Route: Connection between two cities. 2. Train Car: Colored card or plastic train. 3. Destination Ticket: Card showing two cities.



Variations: 1. Team Play: Partners work together. 2. Europe: Different map and rules. 3. Alvin & Dexter: Introduces characters with special abilities.



Tips and Strategies: 1. Focus on completing Destination Tickets. 2. Block opponents' routes. 3. Build efficient train routes. 4. Manage resources.
