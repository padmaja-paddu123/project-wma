RULES OF Base ball

Here's a comprehensive overview of the rules of baseball: Gameplay Basics 1. Objective: Score more runs than the opposing team by hitting a ball with a bat and running around the four bases. 2. Game duration: 9 innings, with each team getting a chance to bat and field. 3. Teams: Two teams, each with 9 players.



Basic Rules 1. Innings: Each team plays offense (bats) and defense (fields) in each inning. 2. At-bats: Each player gets a chance to hit the ball. 3. Strikes: Three strikes result in an out. 4. Balls: Four balls outside the strike zone result in a walk. 5. Hits: Ball hit into play, allowing runner to reach base. 6. Runs: Scored when a runner reaches home plate.



Common Rules 1. Outs: Three outs end the inning. - Strikeout (swinging or called) - Flyout (ball caught in air) - Groundout (ball fielded, runner out) - Tag out (runner touched with ball) - Force out (runner forced to advance) 2. Base Running: - Runners can advance on hits or errors. - Runners must touch each base. - Runners can be tagged or forced out. 3. Fielding: - Fielders must catch or field the ball. - Fielders can throw to bases to record outs. 4. Pitching: - Pitchers throw from the pitcher's mound. - Pitchers must follow specific rules (e.g., stepping off the rubber).





Advanced Rules 1. Lead-offs: Runners can't leave base until ball is pitched. 2. Stealing: Runners can attempt to steal bases. 3. Bunting: Hitting the ball softly to avoid swinging. 4. Double plays: Fielders record two outs on one play. 5. Infield fly rule: Prevents fielders from intentionally dropping fly balls. 6. Designated hitter (DH): Used in American League, allows team to designate hitter instead of pitcher.



Miscellaneous Rules 1. Rain delays: Games can be delayed or called due to weather. 2. Tie games: Games can end in a tie after 9 innings (regular season). 3. Extra innings: Played until one team leads after an inning. 4. Ejections: Players or coaches can be ejected for unsportsmanlike conduct.



Key Positions 1. Pitcher (P) 2. Catcher (C) 3. First baseman (1B) 4. Second baseman (2B) 5. Third baseman (3B) 6. Shortstop (SS) 7. Left fielder (LF) 8. Center fielder (CF) 9. Right fielder (RF)

Common Penalties 1. Balks (pitcher's illegal motion) 2. Interference (runner or fielder obstructs play) 3. Unsportsmanlike conduct This summary covers the basics and some advanced rules. Baseball has many nuances, but understanding these rules provides a solid foundation.
