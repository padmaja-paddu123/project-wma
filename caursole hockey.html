RULES OF Hockey

Hockey is an exciting sport with a rich history, and understanding its rules can enhance your enjoyment of the game. The objective of hockey is simple: score more goals than the opposing team by using a stick to drive the ball or puck into the opponent's goal ¹ ².



Gameplay Basics - Each team has 11 players, including a goalkeeper, and five substitutes ¹. - The game is divided into four quarters, each lasting 15 minutes, with a two-minute break after the first and third quarters and a 15-minute halftime break ¹. - Players use sticks to pass, dribble, and shoot the ball, and goals can only be scored from inside the striking circle ¹.



Penalties and Restarts - A penalty corner is awarded when a foul occurs within the striking circle, allowing the opposing team to score from a set play ¹. - A penalty stroke, or penalty flick, is awarded when a foul prevents a certain goal from being scored, resulting in a one-on-one situation with the goalkeeper ¹. - Free hits are awarded when a foul occurs outside the striking circle, allowing the opposing team to restart play ¹.



Player Positions and Roles - Forwards are responsible for scoring goals, while defensemen focus on preventing opponents from scoring ². - Goalkeepers are the only players allowed to touch the ball with any part of their body and have special privileges and rules ².

Common Infractions - Offside: when an attacking player crosses the blue line before the puck ². - Icing: when a team shoots the puck from behind their own blue line and across the opponent's goal line without the puck being touched by another player ².
