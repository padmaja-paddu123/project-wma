RULES OF Tennis

Here's a comprehensive overview of the rules of tennis: Objective: Hit the ball over the net into the opponent's court while preventing the opponent from doing the same.

Gameplay Basics: 1. Match: Best 2 out of 3 or 3 out of 5 sets. 2. Set: First player to win 6 games, with a 2-game advantage (e.g., 6-4). 3. Game: Player who wins four points with a two-point advantage (e.g., 40-15). 4. Point: Awarded to the player who wins a rally.

Basic Rules: 1. Serve: Player serves into opponent's court diagonally. 2. Rallies: Players hit the ball back and forth until one fails to return. 3. Volleys: Hitting the ball before it bounces. 4. Lets: Ball hits net but still lands in opponent's court; replayed. 5. Faults: Serve lands outside opponent's court or fails to clear net.
Scoring Terms: 1. 15: First point won 2. 30: Second point won 3. 40: Third point won 4. Deuce: 40-40 tie 5. Advantage: Player wins point after deuce 6. Break: Player wins opponent's service game 7. Hold: Player wins own service game

Key Rules: 1. Out of bounds: Ball lands outside court lines. 2. Foot faults: Server's foot touches center mark or baseline. 3. Double faults: Two consecutive faulty serves. 4. Lets and faults: Serve replayed. 5. Time between points: 20 seconds (30 seconds between games). 6. Changing ends: Players switch sides every odd-numbered game.

Advanced Rules: 1. Ace: Serve lands in opponent's court without being touched. 2. Service lets: Ball hits net but still lands in opponent's court. 3. Player interference: Obstruction of opponent's shot. 4. Coaching: Players can receive coaching during changeovers. 5. Challenges: Players can dispute calls using Hawk-Eye technology.

Miscellaneous Rules: 1. Weather delays: Matches can be delayed or postponed. 2. Injuries: Players can receive medical timeouts. 3. Equipment: Players must use approved rackets and balls. 4. Conduct: Players can be penalized for unsportsmanlike behavior.

Key Positions: 1. Server 2. Receiver 3. Baseline player 4. Net player (volleyer)

Common Penalties: 1. Loss of point 2. Loss of game 3. Warning or penalty for unsportsmanlike conduct

Tennis Variations: 1. Singles: One-on-one match 2. Doubles: Two-on-two match 3. Mixed doubles: Male-female pairs
